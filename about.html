About

I am a Ph.D. student at the University of California San Diego. In my research, I develop computational and mathematical tools for simulating acoustic phenomena. For my Ph.D. thesis, I study computational geometric techniques for sound synthesis. Topics of interest include geometry reduction in room acoustics modeling, vortex filament dynamics in aeroacoustics simulations, and collision detection in acoustics systems. My research has applications in computer music, computer graphics, augmented reality, and other fields. My work has been published by The Optical Society and the International Computer Music Association, and it's been featured in consumer products including the Razer Nari Ultimate headsets. My research is advised by Tamara Smyth and Miller Puckette.

Before coming to UC San Diego, I wrote software for Moog Music, ARIA, J.P. Morgan, and Lofelt, and I studied pure and applied physics at Edinburgh, Wake Forest, and BYU. As a software developer, I wrote algorithms for sound synthesizers, sound navigation systems, electronic trading applications, and audio-haptic devices. As a physics student, I conducted research in acoustics, optics, fluid dynamics, and biophysics. Additionally, I taught math and physics to college and high school students. I'm originally from Los Angeles, California, and I currently live in San Diego with my wife and two daughters.
