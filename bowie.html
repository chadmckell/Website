MUS 15: David Bowie

TA: Chad McKell (cmckell@ucsd.edu)
Office hours: By appointment
UCSD, Winter 2021

Meeting 1: Course Overview, Introductions, and David Jones

Monday, January 11, 2021

Today, we'll go over some general course information, introduce ourselves to each other, listen to some music from David Bowie's early days (i.e. the days before he became David "Bowie"), and talk about the Discussion 1 topic. After today's meeting, you should be familiar with the basic structure of the course and come away with some ideas for your Discussion 1 writeup on authenticity.

Meeting 2: The music industry, early influences, miming, and first four albums

Tuesday, January 19, 2021

In today's meeting, we'll first have a conversation about some of the key topics covered in Module 2—the music industry, David Bowie's early influences, Bowie's miming work, and Bowie's first four albums (David Bowie, David Bowie–revived, The Man Who Sold the World, and Hunky Dory). Then, we'll talk about the Discussion 2 writeup on creativity.

Module 2 Notes

Meeting 3: Ziggy Stardust—The Plastic Rock Star

Monday, January 25, 2021

We'll first summarize the topics covered in Module 3—the influences on the Ziggy Stardust concept, Bowie’s work as a producer, and Bowie’s next four albums (The Rise and Fall of Ziggy Stardust and the Spiders from Mars, Aladdin Sane, Pin Ups, and Diamond Dogs). Then, we'll go over the Discussion 3 writeup on cover songs. To get you thinking, we'll listen to some well-known (and not so well-known) cover songs.
